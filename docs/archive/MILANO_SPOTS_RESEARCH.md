## Key Locations Identified

### 1. Leoncavallo CSOA (Via Watteau 7, Greco)
**Category**: Historic social center, protected heritage
**Coordinates**: 45.4967, 9.2081
**Status**: Protected by Milano Superintendent's Office

Defined by art critic Vittorio Sgarbi as "the contemporary Sistine Chapel". Features graffiti dating back to 2003 from the 9th International Happening of Underground Art (HIU). Works by Blu, Ozmo, Atomo, Pao, Mr. Wany, Zed1, Tv Boy, Bros, Tawa, Zibe. Founded 1975, occupied Via Watteau since 1994.

### 2. Cox18 CSOA (Via Conchetta 18, Navigli/Ticinese)
**Category**: Historic social center, iconic Blu mural
**Coordinates**: 45.4389, 9.1803
**Status**: Occupied, famous piece

Entire facade painted by Blu in 2008 with dense texture of stylized characters. One of Milano's most iconic street art pieces. Houses Primo Moroni archive and Calusca bookshop. Squatted since 1976.

### 3. Via Pontano (NoLo district)
**Category**: Free wall, hall of fame
**Coordinates**: 45.4506, 9.2105
**Status**: Free (Comune Milano initiative)

Milano's "East Side Gallery" - murals numbered like museum artworks. One of 100 free walls by Milano Municipality. TDK crew hall of fame. Stretches from Via Padova to Viale Monza, almost uninterrupted to Lambrate metro. Best place to paint in Milano.

### 4. Giardino delle Culture (Via Morosini 8)
**Category**: Public park, famous Millo murals
**Coordinates**: 45.4618, 9.1893
**Status**: Occupied, high-quality street art

Two walls by Millo: "Love Seeker" and "Heart Slingshot". Public park near Piazza Cinque Giornate. One of Milano's most photographed murals.

### 5. Ortica Neighborhood (Quartiere Ortica)
**Category**: Open-air museum, 20+ large murals
**Coordinates**: 45.4264, 9.1812
**Status**: Occupied, permanent installation

OR.ME (Ortica Memoria) project - 20 murals among the largest in Italy telling Milano's 20th century history. Created by Orticanoodles duo (Walli & Alita), pioneers of Italian stencil art. Via Ortica 12 and 16 host iconic pieces. Entire neighborhood transformed into open-air museum.

### 6. Via Schiaffino (Bovisa, Politecnico area)
**Category**: Free wall, university district
**Coordinates**: 45.5130, 9.1589
**Status**: Free, festival venue

Former hall of fame with new pieces added. Poli Urban Colors festival featuring Peeta, Zedz, 2501, Rancy, Frah Quintale. "Mutevole" by Elisabetta Mastro (400sqm, 23 sections). Some of the largest walls in the neighborhood.

### 7. Via Camillo ed Otto Cima (Lambrate)
**Category**: Hall of fame, historic crew spot
**Coordinates**: 45.4890, 9.2144
**Status**: Free, active writing spot

Long UK/TGF crew hall of fame, their headquarters in the '90s. Industrial area with railway walls and disused sites. Pieces by Styng 253, Virus, Ghen, Thero. Near Piazza Monte Titano with Blu's "giant bicycles vs tiny cars" mural.

### 8. Darsena/Navigli
**Category**: Open-air museum, canal district
**Coordinates**: 45.4510, 9.1804
**Status**: Occupied, mixed legal status

Murals on bridges and canal houses. Famous "Shark of Milano" on Naviglio Pavese bridge. Casa Gorizia (Via Vigevano/Via Gorizia corner) with Tilf style and Vandalo tags. Via Magolfa features Alda Merini museum house with poetry murals.

### 9. Via Borsieri 5 (Isola)
**Category**: Historic Isola mural
**Coordinates**: 45.4896, 9.1900
**Status**: Occupied, landmark piece

Corner Via Sebenico. One of Isola's most historic murals by Microbo, Bo130, The Don. 2021 addition: mural dedicated to Aida Accolla (La Scala prima ballerina) in pink/gray tones. Isola is the birthplace of Milano street art.

### 10. Via Pepe (Isola)
**Category**: Massive community project
**Coordinates**: 45.4902, 9.1910
**Status**: Occupied, landmark

250-meter mural dedicated to Leonardo da Vinci (500th death anniversary). 25 artists including students, citizens, volunteers. Pedestrianized area between Via Pepe and Via Borsieri.

### 11. Via Padova (NoLo - North of Loreto)
**Category**: Multicultural neighborhood murals
**Coordinates**: 45.4620, 9.2098
**Status**: Occupied, community-focused

Multiethnic neighborhood in transformation. Robico (150sqm at Fineco headquarters), Chekos Art. Sef01's "Santa Sarita" (Peruvian sacred/pop iconography). Boris Veliz's Dante in Ecuadorian poncho. Mirrors cultural diversity.

### 12. San Lorenzo Columns Area
**Category**: Historic center, heritage dialogue
**Coordinates**: 45.4638, 9.1821
**Status**: Occupied, cultural project

"Milano Street History" project with historical figures: Giuseppe Verdi, Napoleone, Attila, Sant'Ambrogio. Street art dialoguing with cultural heritage near San Lorenzo basilica and columns. University and nightlife area.

### 13. Famagosta Underpass (Isola borders)
**Category**: Underground passage, pure writing
**Coordinates**: 45.4850, 9.2050
**Status**: Free, authentic underground

Pedestrian underpass beneath Viale Famagosta overpass. Strong presence of tags and throw-ups. For writers interested in lettering and pure graffiti writing. Authentic underground atmosphere.

### 14. Via Viotti (Lambrate)
**Category**: Functional street art, anti-smog
**Coordinates**: 45.4398, 9.1756
**Status**: Occupied, innovative

Large-scale anti-smog mural using Airlite paint - absorbs atmospheric pollution, reduces nitrogen dioxide by 90%. Example of functional street art uniting art and environmental sustainability.

### 15. Porto di Mare / Corsico (South Milano)
**Category**: Legal graffiti wall, railway area
**Coordinates**: 45.4392, 9.1710
**Status**: Free, high security

Hall of Fame on railway walls. Disused industrial area. Legal graffiti wall documented on legal-walls.net. CAUTION: railway area, high security, paint only during permitted hours (22:00-06:00). Old school Milano writing tradition.
